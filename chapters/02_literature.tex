\section{Literature Review}

This section provides a comprehensive review of the theoretical foundations and empirical evidence relevant to the intersection of feline behavior and Project Management. The review integrates classical stakeholder theory with modern agile frameworks to explain cat-based project environments.

\subsection{The Metaphor of Herding Cats}
Historically, the phrase "herding cats" has been conceptualized in management literature as a metaphor for managing highly autonomous, non-compliant human professionals \parencite{Vial2019}. However, systematic research has failed to address literal cat herding. Feline stakeholders are characterized by what \textcite{Verhoef2021} describe as "extreme self-governance," meaning they do not recognize human authority, project milestones, or deadlines.

\subsection{Dynamic Feline Capabilities}
To understand feline behavior in a household environment, we draw on the dynamic capabilities framework proposed by \textcite{Teece2007}. We conceptualize feline interactions through three core capabilities:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Sensing:} The ability of a cat to detect the opening of a tuna can from a distance of up to 50 meters, regardless of physical barriers or sleep state.
    \item \textbf{Seizing:} The immediate physical occupation of any newly placed cardboard box, keyboard, or warm laptop.
    \item \textbf{Transforming:} The rapid alteration of a pristine leather sofa into shredded fibers within minutes.
\end{enumerate}
These capabilities are crucial for felines to maintain their strategic dominance in the household economy \parencite{WarnerWager2019}.

\subsection{Feline Resilience and External Disruptions}
Organizational resilience, defined by \textcite{Duchek2020} as the capability to anticipate, cope with, and adapt to unexpected changes, is highly developed in felines. Feline resilience is built upon two distinct coping mechanisms:
\begin{description}
    \item[The Napping Routine:] Felines maintain high robustness by dedicating up to 18 hours per day to preventive rest \parencite{Denyer2017}. This ensures optimal energy reserves for high-priority sprints (such as the 3 AM zoomies).
    \item[The Vacuum Avoidance Strategy:] When confronted with extreme environmental noise (e.g., vacuum cleaner activation), feline stakeholders employ an immediate flight protocol, seeking high-elevation hiding locations to mitigate risk.
\end{description}
Understanding these behavioral patterns is key to developing a successful Feline Stakeholder Management (FSM) framework.
