\section{Feline Survey Instrument}
\label{app:survey}

This appendix presents the complete survey instrument used to assess feline stakeholder satisfaction. Since cats cannot fill out forms, the questions were administered via direct observation of tail-wagging, ear-positioning, and meow-pitch indicators.

\subsection{Section A: Feline Demographics}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item What is your primary sleeping location?
    \begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
        \item Cardboard box (any size)
        \item Human's laptop keyboard (preferably active)
        \item Freshly washed and folded black laundry
        \item Expensive orthopedic cat bed (never used)
    \end{enumerate}

    \item How do you classify your breed background?
    \begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
        \item Purebred royalty (e.g., Persian, Maine Coon)
        \item Domestic Short/Longhair (also known as the "Standard Tabby")
        \item Feral neighborhood ruler
    \end{enumerate}

    \item What is your current role in the household?
    \begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
        \item Silent dictator
        \item Vocal food-demander (3 AM shift)
        \item Sofa destruction specialist
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Section B: Treat Motivation Scale}
\noindent\textit{Please indicate your level of agreement with each statement based on your behavior (1 = Immediate walk-away, 7 = Frantic head-butting and purring).}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item I am willing to sit on command for a dry kibble treat.
    \item I am willing to perform complex acrobatic tricks for a lickable wet treat.
    \item The sound of a food bag shaking justifies waking up from a deep sleep.
    \item I will pretend to be starving even if my bowl was filled 10 minutes ago.
    \item[] \vdots
    \item[] \textit{(Items 6--10 follow the same observation guidelines)}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Section C: Human Annoyance Scale}
\noindent\textit{Please indicate your level of irritation toward the human project manager (1 = Purring through pets, 7 = Direct swipe at ankles).}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The human attempts to rub my belly for longer than 3 seconds.
    \item The human closes a door, restricting my access to a room I have no intention of entering.
    \item The human places a phone call during my scheduled napping hours.
    \item The human attempts to remove me from the keyboard while I am editing their code.
\end{enumerate}
